The Web2 vs Web3 Privacy Paradox

Web2 brought us platforms, cloud computing, and global connectivity, but one principle was absolutely non-negotiable: privacy. Banking runs on SWIFT's encrypted networks, healthcare operates under HIPAA's strict guidelines, and payments flow through encrypted rails that protect every transaction.

If encryption was essential for Web2's success, why would any institution adopt a blockchain where every transaction, strategy, and client interaction is public by default?

The Reality Check: Institutional privacy norms already exist and work.
